# Story: The Customer Journey

**Topic:** Customer Journey — introductory lesson, HBO Business Administration year 1
**Cards:** 8
**Estimated swipe time:** ~2 minutes

---

## Card 1 — Hook

**Headline:** You've been on hundreds of customer journeys. You just didn't notice.

**Body:** Every time you discovered a brand, compared options, bought something, came back — or told a friend — you were on one.

**Prompt:** Think of the last thing you bought online. How did you find it?

**Visual direction:** Person scrolling on a phone late at night, warm screen glow, mid-purchase moment. Mood: recognisable, slightly hypnotic. No text overlay needed — the headline carries it.

---

## Card 2 — Situation

**Headline:** Lotte just started her first marketing job. Her manager's first question stopped her cold.

**Body:** "Show me our customer journey." Lotte had heard the term in class. But now someone actually needed the answer.

**Visual direction:** Young woman at a laptop in an open-plan office, looking slightly puzzled. Mood: grounded, relatable, a little uncertain. Clean and realistic — not staged.

---

## Card 3 — Tension

**Headline:** Great product. Good reviews. And still — most customers never came back.

**Body:** Lotte digs into the data. New customers come in. Then they disappear. The company keeps spending on ads to find new ones. Something is wrong with the journey.

**Prompt:** Where do you think it's going wrong?

**Visual direction:** Simple flat-line graph showing one purchase spike and then nothing. Mood: unsettled, something missing. Minimalist data visual on a dark or muted background.

---

## Card 4 — Concept

**Headline:** The purchase is phase 3. There are 5.

**Body:** Bewustwording → Overweging → Aankoop → Retentie → Ambassadeurschap. Most companies pour energy into the first three. The last two are where loyalty — and real growth — actually happen.

**Visual direction:** Clean horizontal arc showing 5 phases as labelled steps. Phase 3 (Aankoop) highlighted but clearly in the middle — not at the end. Mood: precise, clarifying. Simple iconography per phase.

---

## Card 5 — Concept

**Headline:** Every time a customer touches your brand, they form an opinion. Every single time.

**Body:** A touchpoint is any moment of contact — an Instagram ad, a delivery box, a chat message, a follow-up email. Each one either builds the relationship or quietly damages it.

**Visual direction:** A horizontal timeline with small icons spread across it: social ad, website, checkout, package, email, review notification. Mix of online and offline. Mood: curious, slightly overwhelming — to make the point that it adds up.

---

## Card 6 — Data

**Headline:** 400 million people shared Spotify's marketing for free. In one month.

**Body:** Every December, Spotify Wrapped turns listening data into a personalised story. Users share it on Instagram, on Twitter, everywhere. Spotify didn't ask them to. They just wanted to.

**Prompt:** Did you share yours?

**Visual direction:** Bold Spotify Wrapped-style typography — vivid gradient background (purple, green, pink), the number 400M front and centre. Mood: surprising, energetic, a little playful. No photo — pure graphic.

---

## Card 7 — Reflection

**Headline:** Think of a brand you'd genuinely recommend to a friend. What made you feel that way?

**Prompt:** Was it the product — or something that happened after you bought it?

**Visual direction:** Person casually texting on a couch, natural light. Mood: warm, personal, unhurried. The question is the whole card — no body text. Simple and open.

---

## Card 8 — Cliffhanger

**Headline:** How do you design a journey that turns a buyer into someone who does your marketing for free?

**Body:** That's what we're figuring out in class.

**Visual direction:** An unfinished path or map — literally trailing off the edge of the screen into white space. Mood: forward-looking, unresolved, inviting. Could be illustrated (a dotted trail on a map) or abstract (a line that stops mid-frame).
