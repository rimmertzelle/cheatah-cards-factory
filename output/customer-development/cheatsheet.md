# Cheatsheet — Customer Journey
**Minor Innovative Entrepreneurship**

---

## Novice
*Full definitions and examples — for students new to this topic*

**Target group**
A defined group of people or organisations that a product, service, or campaign focuses on. They share relevant characteristics — demographic, geographic, psychographic, or behavioural. Example: not just "men aged 30–50" but "men aged 30–50 who are first-time founders anxious about marketing spend."

**Persona**
A fictional but research-based character that represents a type of real user. You give your target group a face, a name, and a full description to bring your knowledge to life. A persona is based on facts from interviews and research — not on assumptions. It is dynamic: you keep improving it as you learn more.

**Empathy map**
A tool for describing a persona beyond demographics. It captures what the customer thinks, feels, sees, hears, says, and does while interacting with a product or service. Goal: create genuine empathy and see the world through the customer's eyes.

**Customer experience**
The sum of every interaction a customer has with a business — cognitive, emotional, behavioural, sensorial, and social. It is shaped by every touchpoint across the entire purchase journey, not just the moment of sale.

**Buyer journey**
The process a prospect goes through to *become* a customer. Phases: Awareness (noticing a need), Consideration (comparing solutions), Decision (making a purchase). Focus: acquisition. Content strategy targets potential customers who do not yet know or trust the brand.

**Customer journey**
The process of nurturing *existing* customers to retain their business and grow loyalty. Focus: retention, customer lifetime value (CLV), and advocacy. Requires a different strategy, different content, and different touchpoints than the buyer journey.

**Touchpoint**
Any interaction — including moments with no direct contact — that can change how a customer feels about a brand, product, or service. Four types:

- **Brand-owned** — your website, ads, packaging, customer service
- **Customer-owned** — reviews, word-of-mouth, personal recommendations
- **Partner-owned** — resellers, affiliates, third-party platforms
- **Social/external** — press, Reddit, influencer content, comparison sites

**Moment of Truth (MOT)**
A moment when a customer interacts with a brand and forms or changes an impression.

- **ZMOT (Zero MOT)** — happens online *before* any contact with the brand: Google searches, reviews, social media. The customer has already formed an opinion before you know they exist.
- **FMOT (First MOT)** — the first direct encounter: seeing the product on a shelf or landing on your site.
- **SMOT (Second MOT)** — the experience *after* purchase: does the product deliver on its promise?

**Customer journey versions**

- **1.0** — built on your current desk research and assumptions
- **2.0** — built on real customer insights from interviews and data
- **3.0** — the ideal journey based on recommendations and improvements

## Mediate
*Key terms and short definitions — for students with some prior knowledge*

**Target group** — A defined group sharing relevant characteristics that a product or campaign focuses on. Must be specific enough to guide decisions.

**Persona** — A research-based fictional character representing a type of user. Built on facts, not intuition. Dynamic — updated as new research comes in.

**Empathy map** — A tool to describe a persona's thoughts, feelings, behaviours, and environment beyond demographics.

**Customer experience** — The total of all interactions a customer has with a business across the full purchase journey.

**Buyer journey** — The path from stranger to customer (Awareness → Consideration → Decision). Focus: acquisition.

**Customer journey** — The path from customer to loyal advocate. Focus: retention, CLV, and word-of-mouth. Requires a different strategy than the buyer journey.

**Touchpoint** — Any interaction that shapes how a customer feels about a brand. Four types: brand-owned, customer-owned, partner-owned, social/external.

**ZMOT** — Zero Moment of Truth: the online research a customer does before any contact with the brand. Often the most decisive moment.

**FMOT / SMOT** — First MOT (first direct encounter) and Second MOT (post-purchase experience).

**Journey 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0** — Assumption-based → research-based → ideal/improved journey.

## Expert
*Quick reminders only — for students who already know this topic*

- **Target group** — specific, validated, segmented by relevant criteria
- **Persona** — research-based, dynamic, not assumption-driven
- **Empathy map** — beyond demographics: thinks, feels, sees, does
- **Customer experience** — all touchpoints, full journey, every impression
- **Buyer journey** — stranger → customer; acquisition focus
- **Customer journey** — customer → advocate; retention + CLV focus
- **Touchpoints** — brand / customer / partner / social — you own only some
- **ZMOT** — pre-contact research; opinion formed before you show up
- **FMOT / SMOT** — first encounter / post-purchase experience
- **Journey versions** — 1.0 assumptions → 2.0 research → 3.0 ideal
