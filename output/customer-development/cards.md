# Story: The Customer Journey

**Topic:** Customer Journey — Minor Innovative Entrepreneurship
**Cards:** 8
**Estimated swipe time:** ~2 minutes

---

## Card 1 — Hook

**Headline:** Your customer made their decision before they ever found you.

**Body:** Before your campaign launched, before they saw your ad, before they visited your site — they'd already googled you, read what others wrote, and formed an opinion. That moment is called the Zero Moment of Truth. You weren't there for it.

**Visual direction:** Phone screen showing a Google search results page with star ratings and reviews. Mood: slightly unnerving — the customer is in control, not the brand. No people visible, just the screen.

---

## Card 2 — Situation

**Headline:** Noa spent three weeks building her customer journey. Her team loved it.

**Body:** The slides were colourful. The phases were logical. The touchpoints were carefully mapped. Every single one was based on what Noa imagined her customers would do — not on what they actually do.

**Visual direction:** Someone presenting a large, colourful journey map pinned to a wall. Teammates nodding. Mood: confident, slightly naive. Clean and realistic — not staged.

---

## Card 3 — Tension

**Headline:** Then she interviewed her first real customer. None of it matched.

**Body:** The customer found Noa's brand through a Reddit thread she didn't know existed. They almost didn't buy because of a review she had never read. The journey Noa had mapped was a story she told herself.

**Prompt:** How well do you actually know your own customer's path?

**Visual direction:** A printed journey map with sticky notes being moved, crossed out, or added in pen. Mood: unsettled, something is being taken apart. The map looks less certain than before.

---

## Card 4 — Concept

**Headline:** There's a buyer journey and a customer journey. Most people only build one.

**Body:** The buyer journey is about turning strangers into customers — awareness, consideration, purchase. The customer journey is about turning customers into loyal advocates — retention, CLV, word-of-mouth. Different people, different goals, different strategies entirely.

**Visual direction:** Two clearly separate paths branching from a single purchase moment. One path labelled "buyer" leading left, one labelled "customer" leading right. Minimalist, clean. Mood: clarifying.

---

## Card 5 — Concept

**Headline:** The touchpoints that matter most aren't yours.

**Body:** Your brand controls some touchpoints — your ads, your website, your packaging. But reviews, Reddit posts, and a friend's recommendation are written by others. Those often tip the decision. You can influence them. You cannot own them.

**Visual direction:** Four labelled touchpoint clusters arranged in a loose orbit: brand-owned (small), partner-owned (medium), customer-owned (medium), social/external (large and furthest from centre). Mood: slightly overwhelming — the brand's control is smaller than expected.

---

## Card 6 — Concept

**Headline:** Before your customer walks in, they've already decided.

**Body:** The Zero Moment of Truth happens on Google, in WhatsApp groups, in comment sections — before any direct contact with your brand. It's where opinions form and comparisons are made. If you're not present in that moment, someone else is.

**Visual direction:** Person scrolling on their phone in bed at night, reading reviews, comparing two options side by side. Mood: intimate, decisive. This is a private moment — the brand has no seat at the table.

---

## Card 7 — Reflection

**Headline:** Most purchase decisions are made before the customer ever contacts the brand.

**Prompt:** Think of the last time you chose one product over another. Was the decision already made by the time you reached the brand?

**Visual direction:** Split image — two identical products, one surrounded by reviews, ratings, and recommendations; one without. Mood: calm, personal. The difference between them is invisible to the brand.

---

## Card 8 — Cliffhanger

**Headline:** Noa's journey is version 1.0 — built on what she imagined. How do you build version 2.0?

**Body:** A 1.0 journey is built on assumptions. A 2.0 journey is built on research. The gap between them is everything you don't yet know about your customer.

**Visual direction:** Two versions of a journey map side by side — one clean and confident, one covered in question marks, blank spaces, and handwritten notes. Mood: forward-looking, unfinished. The second map is more honest than the first.
